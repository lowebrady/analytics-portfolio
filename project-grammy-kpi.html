Grammy.com KPI &
Engagement Analysis

Used advanced Excel analytics to calculate user engagement KPIs and mobile visitor rates for Grammy.com — assessing the measurable business impact of the Recording Academy's 2022 website split and delivering executive-ready findings that earned a Data Analysis Certification.

Role Web Analytics Analyst
Tools Excel — PivotTables, XLOOKUP, SUMIFS, AVERAGEIFS
Domain Web Analytics / Media & Entertainment
Context Global Career Accelerator — Recording Academy / MSU
01 — Background & Business Context

A major site restructuring with measurable consequences

In 2022, the Recording Academy — the organization behind the Grammy Awards — made a significant strategic decision: splitting Grammy.com into two distinct properties. This kind of major digital restructuring carries real business risk. Traffic can fragment. Users who were accustomed to a single destination may not follow a redirect to a new URL. Mobile visitors, who behave differently from desktop users, may drop off entirely.

The question this analysis was designed to answer: did the 2022 website split help or hurt Grammy.com's digital engagement — and what do the KPIs tell us about how users responded? Understanding that impact matters both for evaluating the original decision and for informing any future digital strategy work.

"Web analytics is only useful if it connects to a business decision. The question was never 'what happened to traffic' — it was 'what does the traffic data tell us about whether the website split achieved its goals.'"

02 — Methodology

Advanced Excel as a serious analytical tool

This project used Excel as the primary analytical environment — applying functions well beyond basic formulas to build a rigorous KPI framework from raw web analytics data.

  • 01
    KPI Framework Definition Established the specific engagement metrics to track before touching the data: sessions, bounce rate, pages per session, average session duration, mobile visit share, and return visitor rate. Defining metrics in advance prevents the temptation to cherry-pick favorable numbers after the fact.
  • 02
    XLOOKUP for Cross-Sheet Data Retrieval Used XLOOKUP to pull matching records across multiple sheets — linking traffic data to page-level metadata and device-type breakdowns without manually copying between tables. XLOOKUP's flexibility over VLOOKUP was essential here for matching on non-leftmost columns and handling lookup errors gracefully.
  • 03
    SUMIFS & AVERAGEIFS for Segmented KPIs Applied SUMIFS and AVERAGEIFS with multiple criteria to calculate engagement metrics by device type, date range, and content category simultaneously. This allowed direct comparison of mobile vs. desktop engagement rates before and after the site split — a more meaningful comparison than aggregate averages alone.
  • 04
    PivotTables for Trend Visualization Built PivotTables to summarize monthly engagement trends pre- and post-split, with slicers for device type and content category. The pivot structure made it easy to answer "how did mobile engagement in the awards content category change after the split?" without writing new formulas for each cut of the data.
  • 05
    Executive-Ready Presentation Layer Condensed the full analysis into a summary dashboard using conditional formatting to highlight KPIs above and below pre-split benchmarks. The goal was a deliverable that a non-technical executive could read in under two minutes and understand what changed, why it matters, and what to do about it.
03 — Key Findings

What the KPI data revealed about the split's impact

Mobile

Mobile Visit Rate Analyzed

Calculated mobile visitor share pre- and post-split to determine whether the restructuring disproportionately affected mobile users — who navigate differently and are more sensitive to URL changes and redirect friction.

KPI

Full Engagement Framework

Tracked sessions, bounce rate, pages per session, session duration, and return visitor rate — a complete picture of engagement quality, not just traffic volume, which can be misleading in isolation.

04 — Excel Skills Demonstrated

Why advanced Excel still matters

Excel proficiency is the single most universally required skill in data analyst job postings. This project demonstrates the functions that separate a competent Excel user from an advanced one:

  • ✓
    XLOOKUP — Excel's modern replacement for VLOOKUP, supporting bidirectional lookups, returning multiple columns, and handling errors without nested IFERROR wrapping. Used here to link data across sheets without manual data entry.
  • ✓
    SUMIFS / AVERAGEIFS — Multi-criteria conditional aggregation. The core function for segmented KPI calculation — summing or averaging only the rows that match multiple conditions simultaneously. Essential for any analysis that needs to slice data by more than one dimension.
  • ✓
    PivotTables with slicers — Rapid exploratory summarization that doesn't require rewriting formulas for each new cut of the data. Slicers make pivot tables accessible to non-technical stakeholders who need to explore the data themselves.
  • ✓
    Conditional formatting for executive dashboards — Visual encoding that makes the direction of KPI changes immediately apparent. Green/red formatting on a benchmark comparison table communicates "better/worse" faster than any chart can.
